CSIR-NET Physical Sciences: Classical Mechanics Syllabus

1. Newtonian Mechanics

  • Newton’s laws of motion, inertial frames
  • Central forces, conservative forces
  • Motion under central forces (Kepler’s problem)
  • Two-body collisions (elastic & inelastic)
  • Rotating frames, Coriolis and centrifugal forces
  • Rigid body dynamics, moment of inertia tensor

2. Lagrangian and Hamiltonian Mechanics

  • Principle of least action, Lagrange’s equations
  • Constraints (holonomic and non-holonomic)
  • Generalized coordinates and momenta
  • Hamilton’s equations, Poisson brackets
  • Canonical transformations
  • Symmetries and conservation laws (Noether’s theorem)

3. Small Oscillations and Normal Modes

  • Coupled oscillations
  • Eigenfrequencies and normal modes
  • Damped and forced oscillations

4. Special Theory of Relativity

  • Lorentz transformations
  • Relativistic kinematics (time dilation, length contraction)
  • Relativistic dynamics (energy-momentum relation)
  • Four-vectors and covariance

5. Continuum Mechanics (Basics)

  • Waves in continuous media
  • Sound waves, dispersion relations
  • Stress and strain tensors (elementary concepts)

6. Scattering Theory (Classical)

  • Rutherford scattering
  • Cross-section calculations (classical)

Important Topics for CSIR-NET

  • Most Weightage: Lagrangian & Hamiltonian mechanics, central forces, rigid body dynamics.
  • Mathematical Tools: Variational calculus, tensor analysis, differential equations.
  • Problem-Solving Focus:
    • Kepler’s laws & orbital motion
    • Normal modes of coupled oscillators
    • Relativistic collisions & energy-momentum
    • Poisson brackets & canonical transformations

Books Recommended for Preparation

  1. Goldstein – Classical Mechanics (Standard reference)
  2. Landau & Lifshitz – Mechanics (Concise & rigorous)
  3. Kleppner & Kolenkow – Introduction to Mechanics (For basics)
  4. David Morin – Classical Mechanics (Problem-solving focus)

Exam Pattern Relevance

  • Part B: ~2–3 questions from Classical Mechanics (short-answer type).
  • Part C: ~1–2 in-depth problems (often on Lagrangians/Hamiltonians or central forces).
